Ratatouille

Ingridients

For the tomato sauce

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3 gloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and paper to taste

For the Ratatouille

  • 1 eggplant
  • 1 zucchini
  • 1 yellow squash
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and paper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the tomato sauce

    In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic, and sauté until they become soft and translucent. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, dried basil, dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer the sauce over low heat for about 15-20 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Set the sauce aside.

  2. Prepare the vegetables

    Slice the eggplant, zucchini, yellow squash, red bell pepper, yellow bell pepper, and tomatoes into thin rounds of approximately the same thickness. This will ensure even cooking.

  3. Assemble the ratatouille

    Preheat your oven to 190°C (375°F). Take a round baking dish or a cast-iron skillet and spread a thin layer of tomato sauce on the bottom. Arrange the sliced vegetables in an alternating pattern around the dish, starting from the outer edge and working your wat towards the center. Repeat this pattern until the dish is filled. Drizzle olive oil over the assembled vegetables and sprinkle minced garlic, dried thyme, salt, and pepper on top.

  4. Bake the ratatouille

    Cover the baking dish with foil and place it in the preheated oven. Bake for 40-45 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender and cooked through. Remove the foil during the last 10 minutes of baking to allow the vegetables to slightly brown.

  5. Garnish and serve

    Once the ratatouille is cooked, remove it from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh basil leaves for an extra touch of flavor and presentation. Serve the ratatouille as a main dish, a side dish, or even as a vegetarian centerpiece.

Tarte Tatin

Ingridients

For the crust

  • 125g all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 125g unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 15ml lemon juice

For the Tarte Tatin

  • 6 medium Honeycrisp apples
  • 30ml lemon juice
  • 75g unsalted butter
  • 200g sugar
  • 1 vanilla bean, halved and scraped
  • 1 teaspoon fleur de sel (sea salt flakes)

Instructions

  • Make the crust

    1. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar and salt.
    2. Add the cubed butter and mix the ingredients together using your hands (rub between your palms) until you get a crumbly texture and pea-size bits of butter are still visible.
    3. In a small bowl, whisk together with a fork the egg yolk and lemon juice, and add to the other ingredients.
    4. Combine until the dough comes together into a ball. If it feels too dry, you can add a 1 tablespoon of water.
    5. Wrap in plastic film and chill until needed. (min 30 min, max 24).

  • Make the pie

    1. Peel, core and cut the apples in quarters. As you go, reserve the apple quarters in a large bowl with 30ml of lemon juice, to prevent them from browning.
    2. Stir the apple quarters as you go to make sure they're always lightly coated in lemon juice.
    3. In a 9-10 (22.9-25.4cm) cast iron skillet, melt the butter over medium heat, with the sugar and vanilla bean scrapings. Stir and cook for about 5 min, until the sugar has dissolved and the liquid starts to simmer. Add the apple quarters, stir to coat and turn the heat down to low.
    4. Cover with a large sheet of foil (so the steam is kept inside) and cook for 20 min until the apples are tender (but not mushy), stirring occasionally and pouring the liquid in the pan on top of the apples (always place the foil back).
    5. With a slotted spoon or spatula, transfer the apple quarters to a cooling rack and sprinkle evenly with the sea salt flakes. Bring the heat to high and simmer the remaining liquid until it turns into a golden caramel.
    6. Remove from the heat.
    7. Turn your oven to 220°C (430°F)
    8. Take the crust out of the fridge, transfer it to a lightly floured working surface and roll it out to a 30.5cm (12-inch) circle.
    9. When cool enough to handle with your fingers, place the apple quarters back into the skillet (over top the caramel) in a circular pattern, with the "outside of the fruit" side dowm.
    10. Cover with the crust and tuck the edges of the crust into the pan. Poke four tiny holes into the crust with a knife (for the steam to escape while baking).
    11. Bake for 10 min in the pre-heated oven. Turn the oven down to 190°C (375°F) and bake for 20 min,, until the crust is golden.
    12. Transfer the tart to a cooling rack for 5 min, and flip upside down onto a serving plate (do not wait any longer before you flip it, or the caramel will harden and the apples will remain stuck to the bottom of the pan). Use oven gloves or kitchen towels to protect your hands when you flip the tart as some remaining juice may still spill on the side of the skillet.
    13. Serve warm (commonly with dollop of crème fraiche on top), or chilled.

Boeuf Bourguignon

Ingridients

For the beef

  • 1.1 - 1.4kg stewing beef, cubed
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 2 teaspoons of fresh ground black pepper
  • 4 tablespoons of unsalted butter, divided
  • 227 g cured pork or 5-6 slices bacon, cut in fine strips
  • 4 sprigs thyme
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 3 garlic cloves, peeled and finely chopped
  • 10 pearl onions, peeled
  • 4-5 medium carrots, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 500ml red wine
  • 30ml brandy
  • 500ml beef broth
  • 50g romato paste
  • 32g flour
  • Optional: 6-7 sprigs parsley, for garnish

For the mushrooms

  • 28.5g unsalted butter
  • 450g cremini mushrooms, quartered
  • ¼ tsp salt
  • ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper

Instructions

  • Step 1

    About 2 hours before cooking, pat the beef cubes dry with paper towels and season them well with salt and black pepper. Let rest to near room temperature.

  • Step 2

    On the stove top, over medium-high heat, melt 2 tablespoons of butter in a Dutch-Oven. Add the bacon strips and cook for about 6-7 minutes, strirring occasionally, until fully cooked and lightly crisp. Set aside, leaving the fat drippings in the Dutch-Oven.

  • Step 3

    Pre-heat your oven to 163°C (325°F) with a rack in the middle.

  • Step 4

    Working in batches, add the beef cubes to the Dutch-Oven and brown them, about 3 minutes on each side. Be careful not to overcrowd or overlap any meat cubes or they won't brown properly. It should take about 3 to 4 batches. Transfer browned beef to a separate bowl or plate.

  • Step 5

    Melt 2 tablespoons of butter in the Dutch-Oven and add the garlic, carrots, pearl onions and bay leaves. Cook for 6-7 minutes, until the onions are glistering.

  • Step 6

    Add the beef and bacon back in the Dutch-Oven. Add tomato paste and sprinkle with the flour. Stir all the ingredients until no dry flour is visible. Pour in the red wine, brandy and beef stock so the meat is barely covered. Bring to a simmer, cover with a lid and cook for 1 hour 30 minutes in the oven.

  • Step 7 - While the stew cooks in the oven

    Melt 2 tablespoons of butter in a large frying pan over medium heat. Add the mushrooms, season to taste with salt and black pepper, and cook for about 10 minutes, until the mushrooms are cooked and lightly browned.

  • Step 8

  • Take the Beef Bourguignon out of the oven and stir in the mushrooms. Return the pot to the oven for 30 more minutes. Take the Beef Bourguignon out of the oven, adjust seasoning if needed (salt, pepper). Let the Beef Bourguingnon sit for 15 minutes before serving

Blanquette de Veau

(Old Fashioned Veal Stew)

Ingridients

  • 900g veal (best cuts: breast, neck, shoulder, rib roast), cut into 5cm chunks
  • 2 liters water
  • 15 grams salt
  • 1 rib celery
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig thyme
  • 1 onion, studded with 1 clove
  • 3 carrots, peeled and sliced diagonally
  • 12 baby onions
  • 90 grams unsalted butter, divided
  • 12 small white mushrooms
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • 25 grams all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon grated nutmeg
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 120ml heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. In a large Dutch oven over medium heat high heat, heat the veal, water, and salt to boil then reduce to a simmer and let cook for 30 minutes. You will notice that a lot of scum will float to the surface. Use a spoon or a ladle to skim it off and throw it away
  2. Cut the celery in half and sandwich your bay leaf and thyme in between. Tie with a piece of kitchen twine or string. Add the celery along with the studded onion and carrots. Let the veal slowly cook until the meat is insanely tender, about 1 hour.
  3. While the veal is cooking, boil the small onions in boiling water for 2 minutes, or until the skins just pop off with the slightest pressure. Drain and remove the skins. Cook the little onions in a tablespoon of butter mixed with 60g of veal broth. Let cook for 5 minutes, or until the onions are tender. Let them sit until you are ready to finish the dish.
  4. Trim the ends off of your cleaned mushrooms. Melt 1 tablespoon of butter in a small skillet. Add the mushrooms and cook gently being careful not to color them at all. Add the lemon juice and a ladle full of veal broth and let the mushrooms cook for 5 minutes, or until soft. Let them sit until you are ready to finish the dish.
  5. When the veal is tender strain the meat, keeping both the meat and the broth. Discard the celery and the onion. Keep the carrots.
  6. Melt the remaining 3 tablespoons of butter in your Dutch oven over low heat. As soon as it is melted add the flour and let cook very gently for 5 minutes. You do not want any color at all.
  7. Slowly add your veal broth to the flour mixture, whisking constantly to prevent any lumps. Add the nutmeg and taste. Season with salt and pepper.
  8. Bring to a boil then return the meat and carrots to the dish along with your small onions and mushrooms.
  9. Whisk together the eggs and cream then pour into the stew. Immediately turn off the heat. The eggs will thicken the broth slightly. The final consistency should be that of a light stew, not overly thick. Sprinkle parsley over and serve immediately.
  10. Serve directly from the pot with rice, boiled potatoes, or even buttered noodles.

Soupe à L’oignon

Ingridients

  • 400g of brown onions
  • 1 litre of stock
  • 50g of top quality unsalted butter
  • 100 to 200g of gruyere or comte cheese (grated)
  • Several slices of French baguette or similar slightly toasted
  • Salt and pepper for the seasoning
  • 1 rib celery
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 sprig thyme
  • Several sprigs of parsley
  • 1 sprig leek

Instructions

  1. Peel, wash and finely slice the onions (or use a food processor).
  2. Make a bouquet garni (with leek, celery, thyme,1 bay leaf and parsley tied together)
  3. Heat up the stock (homemade or top quality ready-made stock) and leave to simmer.
  4. Melt the butter in a cast iron or thick bottom pan and add the onions. Stir well and season with salt and pepper. Then cook the onions on a medium heat for 30 minutes until brown (stir regularly to so the onions are evenly browned).
  5. Then add the stock a little at a time stirring gently. Add the bouquet garni and let the soup cook on a low heat for 20 minutes.
  6. Take a small bowl or dish (preferably with handles) and place the baguette slices on the bottom. Pour in some soup (3/4 from the top) and then add a few more slices of bread on top.
  7. Cover the bread with a generous amount of comte cheese and place the dish(es) under the grill for a few minutes until the cheese has melted and a crust is formed.
  8. When done serve immediately.

Steak Tartare

Ingridients

  • 225 grams sirloin, beef tenderloin (filet mignon), or top round steak, fresh
  • 1 baguette, thinly sliced on a bias
  • 60 ml olive oil or melted butter
  • 2 tablespoons capers, drained and finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons cornichons, drained and finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh parsley leaves and tender stems, plus extra for garnish
  • 1 small shallot, finely chopped
  • 3 grams (1/2 teaspoon) kosher salt, plus more to taste
  • 1 gram (1/4 teaspoon) freshly ground black pepper, plus more to taste
  • 3 egg yolks, divided
  • 2 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 230°C, with a rack in the upper third.
  2. On a cutting board, freeze the beef until it is firm on the outside but still easily pierced with a knife, about 40 minutes.
  3. Spread baguette slices in a single layer on a rimmed baking sheet.
  4. Drizzle the slices with olive oil or butter and sprinkle with salt and pepper.
  5. Bake on the upper third oven rack until crispy and golden brown, 10-15 minutes (watch closely, as ovens vary).
  6. In a medium mixing bowl, whisk together the capers, cornichons, parsley, shallot, ½ teaspoon salt, ¼ teaspoon pepper, Worcestershire sauce, mustard, lemon juice, and 1 egg yolk.
  7. Using a sharp knife, thinly slice the beef against the grain, then cut the slices into thin strips (6 mm or thinner) and dice finely.
  8. Transfer the diced beef to the bowl with sauce and mix in to evenly coat. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. On a serving plate, pat half of the beef into a round cookie cutter or ramekin. Repeat on a second plate with the remaining half of the beef tartare.
  10. Using the back of a spoon, create a shallow well in the center of each steak tartare.
  11. Place a raw egg yolk in the well of each steak tartare.
  12. Serve immediately with toasted bread, and garnish with extra chopped parsley.

Cassoulet

Ingridients

  • 250g smoked pancetta
  • 300g pork sausages
  • 600g dried haricot beans, soaked overnight in 3 times their volume of water
  • 300g pork shoulder, cut into 1″ cubes
  • 1 celery stick
  • 1 small white or yellow onion
  • 1 large carrot
  • 8 garlic cloves
  • 2 ripe plum tomatoes
  • 25g goose fat or 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 bouquet garni
  • 8 pinches of sea salt
  • 2 pinches of freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 clove, lightly crushed
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice

To finish

  • 2 confit ducks legs
  • 60g goose fat or 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cups plain dried breadcrumbs
  • 1 garlic clove, finely chopped

Instructions

  1. The night before cooking, you need to soak the dried beans overnight. Simply place the beans in a large bowl and cover with 3 times their volume of water.
  2. The next morning, drain the beans completely and discard the soaking water.
  3. Cut up your pancetta into lardons (small cubes). If you buy the smoked pancetta sliced, make sure you ask for it thickly sliced, so the cubes are nice and thick.
  4. Place the drained beans into a large pot, add the lardons, and cover with fresh cold water. Bring it to a boil and blanch for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Drain everything in the pot into a colander and discard the cooking water. Set aside while you prep some other ingredients.
  6. Chop up the pork shoulder into 1″ cubes. Set aside.
  7. Chop up the onion, celery, carrots and tomatoes. Peel the garlic cloves, leaving them whole. At this point, preheat the oven to 120°C (250°F).
  8. Heat the goose fat or olive oil in a 26cm flameproof casserole over medium heat. Add the pork shoulder and brown for about 8 minutes. Lower the heat to low and then add the onions, celery, carrots and garlic cloves.
  9. While that is cooking, in a separate frying pan, brown the sausage for about 6-8 minutes in a little olive oil.
  10. Cut the sausage into chunks. Set aside.
  11. Add the tomatoes and bouquet garni to the casserole and cook slowly for about 5 minutes until the meat and vegetables begin to caramelize.
  12. Add the sausage, beans, and lardons and pour in 1.2 liters water. Bring to a boil and skim off as much scum off the top as you can. Add the salt, pepper, clove and lemon juice. The water will be almost up to the top, but don't worry, it will reduce as it cooks.
  13. Place the casserole in the oven and cook, uncovered, for 2 - 2½ hours, stirring every hour. After 2 hours, check to see if the beans are soft and creamy and if the broth has thickened. If not, you may need to cook it another 30 minutes - 1 hour to get it to this stage.
  14. Take the casserole out of the oven and add the duck legs. Place them underneath the beans and vegetables.
  15. Add the breadcrumbs on top and drizzle the goose fat or olive oil and garlic on top. Cook for an additional 2 hours.
  16. After this time, the crumbs will have browned.

Confit de Canard

Ingridients

(Serves 6 people)

  • 6 duck legs
  • 4 cloves of garlic
  • Sprig of thyme
  • 4 cups of duck fat (available at specialty groceries)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 160°C (320°F).
  2. Season duck legs with salt and pepper. Place in a deep pan, with the duck legs on their side (arranged so that the sides with meat are touching each other). Cover with the warmed duck fat and add thyme and garlic.
  3. Place a smaller sized lid on top of the duck legs (to keep them submerged in the duck fat), and place in the oven. Let cook for 2 hours total, removing at 1 hour to turn over the duck legs.
  4. Remove from the oven (taking care not to burn yourself with the duck fat), and rearrange the duck legs and completely submerge in the duck fat. Let chill until the duck fat solidifies.
  5. Remove the duck legs from the solid duck fat, place in a baking pan and bake at 150°C (300°F) until the skin crisps up, about 15 minutes.

Bouillabaisse

Ingridients

  • 2.3 kg assorted fresh fish, divided into firm- and soft-fleshed varieties
  • 2 crayfish
  • 1 baguette, sliced 1.25 cm thick
  • 120 ml extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 onions, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 4 medium tomatoes, peeled, cored, seeded, and roughly chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled and roughly chopped
  • 2 sprigs fennel fronds
  • 1 dried bay leaf
  • 0.5 grams saffron threads, crumbled
  • 22 grams coarse sea salt
  • 0.5 grams (1/2 teaspoon) freshly ground black pepper, plus more to taste
  • 2-4 sprigs fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped

Instructions

  1. Prep fish: Scale, gut, and wash fish, discarding gills. Keep the heads and other trimmings—they will flavor and thicken the broth. Very small fish can go into the bouillabaisse whole, while larger fish should be cut crosswise into 5 cm pieces, keeping the bones intact. Cut crayfish in half lengthwise.
  2. Preheat oven to 120°C (250°F). Lay bread slices in a single layer on a baking sheet and toast until crisp and dried-out but not brown, about 30 minutes.
  3. In a large Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add onions, tomatoes, garlic, fennel fronds, bay leaf, saffron, salt, and pepper and sauté until softened but not browned, about 5 minutes. Add firm fish and crayfish and stir to combine. Add enough boiling water to cover fish and vegetables, increase heat to high, and bring to a very strong boil.
  4. Boil for 5 minutes, then add the soft fish. Continue to boil until the fish is cooked through and the broth is creamy, about 7 to 10 minutes longer.
  5. Use a slotted spoon to remove fish slices and arrange on a platter. Arrange bread in a deep vegetable dish and pour broth through a fine mesh sieve onto the bread. Moisten the fish with a ladleful of strained broth and sprinkle everything with parsley.

Tartiflette

Ingridients

  • 1.8 kg firm potatoes
  • 900 g reblochon cheese
  • 6 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 300 ml light crème fraîche
  • 180 ml white wine from Savoy (Apremont, Chignin, Roussette, or similar)
  • 4 onions, thinly sliced
  • 280 g smoked bacon lardons
  • 30 ml olive oil
  • 0.5 g (½ teaspoon) freshly grated nutmeg
  • Salt, to taste
  • 0.5 g freshly ground black pepper (or to taste)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C (250°F).
  2. Peel the potatoes and cut into large cubes.
  3. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  4. Sauté the onions until they become translucent, then add the garlic, smoked bacon lardons and potatoes, stirring frequently with a wooden spoon until obtaining a golden color.
  5. Sprinkle with nutmeg, then deglaze the pan with the white wine, and continue cooking for 5 minutes. Set aside.
  6. Coat the bottom and sides of a round, square or rectangular terracotta baking dish with half the crème fraîche, arrange half the sautéed ingredients, season with salt and pepper.
  7. Then, arrange the second half of the sautéed ingredients, and pour over the second half of the crème fraîche. Season with salt and pepper again.
  8. Scrape the rind of the reblochon so that it regains its orange color, and cut it in 2 or 4 in the direction of the thickest part. Lightly season with salt and pepper.
  9. Place the reblochon, crust side up, and flush against the potatoes.
  10. Bake for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the tartiflette is golden brown.
  11. Serve immediately, accompanied by a green salad such lettuce in vinaigrette, and a white wine from Savoy.

Coq au Vin

Ingridients

  • 1.36 kg skin-on, bone-in chicken legs
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 720 ml red wine
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2-4 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 115 g bacon, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 1 carrot, peeled and diced
  • 225 g button mushrooms, trimmed and quartered
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 7.5 g (1½ teaspoons) tomato paste
  • 15 g all-purpose flour
  • 30 g unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 225 g pearl onions, peeled
  • 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped

Instructions

  1. Season the chicken with salt and pepper. In a large bowl, combine the chicken with the wine, bay leaf, and thyme. Cover and marinate at least 30 minutes and up to one day.
  2. In a Dutch oven or large pot set over medium heat, cook the bacon until browned and crispy, about 10 minutes. Remove from heat and use a slotted spoon to transfer bacon to a plate lined with paper towels, reserving the fat in the Dutch oven.
  3. Remove the chicken from the wine marinade and pat dry with paper towels. Reserve the marinade. Return the Dutch oven to the stovetop and heat the bacon fat over medium heat until it shimmers. Add the chicken, skin side down, in a single layer and cook until golden brown, about 5 minutes. Flip and cook until brown on the other side, about 4 more minutes. (Don't overcrowd the chicken—work in batches if necessary, adding a little olive oil if you run out of rendered bacon fat.) Transfer the seared chicken to a plate.
  4. Add the diced onion, carrot, and mushrooms to the Dutch oven and season with salt. Cook until the vegetables are lightly browned, about 8 minutes.
  5. Add the garlic and tomato paste and cook for about 1 minute, until it's fragrant. Add the reserved marinade. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat and reduce liquid to about half. Skim off foam as it appears.
  6. Add the chicken, pearl onions, and half of the bacon. There should be enough liquid to just cover the chicken—if not, add a little water or chicken stock. Cover and simmer over low heat until the chicken is tender, about 1 hour. Uncover, transfer chicken to a plate, and continue to simmer, about 10 minutes.
  7. Meanwhile, whisk the flour and butter together into a smooth paste. Whisk the beurre manié into the stew to thicken. Continue to simmer until the sauce is thick enough to lightly coat the back of a spoon, about 2 more minutes.
  8. Taste and season with salt and pepper if needed. Return the chicken to Dutch oven and simmer until warmed through, no more than 5 minutes. Remove from heat and garnish with remaining bacon and parsley.
